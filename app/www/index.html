connecting
I2S sync error detected. The card's TDM slots can rotate after this — verify your channel mapping in Channel map.
Under-voltage detected. The Pi's firmware is reporting a sagging supply. Use a 5 V / 2.5 A (or better) power supply and a short, thick cable — the Octo draws current the Pi's regulator has to pass through.
Data partition not mounted. /data fell back to a RAM filesystem, so the device works but settings cannot be saved as boot defaults. The SD card's third partition is missing or unreadable.
Monitor audio is dropping out over the connection. The audio is reaching this browser too late to play cleanly — the cause is the network between your browser and the Sound Tester, not the device under test. Move closer to the access point or use a wired connection. The meters, THD+N and the scope read the device directly and are unaffected.

Inputs

Listen L / Listen R send an input to that ear — press both to hear it centred, none to stop it. Inputs sharing an ear are summed at unity, so several loud ones can clip the browser's output; that is the monitor mix only and changes nothing that is measured. Whatever is playing stays sample-aligned across channels.

Gain is digital make-up gain applied the moment the samples arrive, for devices too quiet to read: the meters, spectrum, THD+N, scope, listen streams and anything routed to an output all show the amplified signal. It cannot recover a signal the ADC already clipped, and pushing it past full scale clamps — the peak meter parks at 0.0 dBFS and the scope flat-tops.

Monitor volume unity Applied in the browser, to both ears at once, on everything Listen L / Listen R is playing. Nothing measured changes — the meters, THD+N and the scope are unaffected, and the device never hears it. Past unity it can clip your computer's output, which is your speakers distorting, not the input.
Monitor buffer 150 ms How far ahead the browser queues the monitor audio before playing it. A larger buffer rides out a jittery link and clears the connection dropouts flagged above, at the cost of that much more delay between the device and what you hear. It changes nothing that is measured; the device never hears it.

Noise

Pink uses the Kellett filter, tuned for 44.1 kHz: at 96 kHz the slope tilts by a couple of dB across the band.

Ping (multiroom)

Only tick decays fast enough to give an unambiguous correlation peak. bing and bong ring for many cycles and are meant to be heard, not measured.

Channel map

The Octo's TDM slots can rotate after an I2S sync error, so what the driver calls channel 1 is not always physical input 1. Play Identify on an output and listen, or watch the input meters, then remap here. Slots must stay distinct.
